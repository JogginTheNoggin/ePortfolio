Artifact 2: Raycast 3d Voxel Rendering

3D Graphics Using DDA Raycasting Algorithm

Narrative

For Milestone Three, the artifact in question was an OpenGL 3D scene. The initial development occurred during the summer of this year for my graphics programming class. However, the improvement is more of a complete overhaul than a simple update. Rasterization was the rendering technique initially used, which, in short, involves processing vertices and triangles. In contrast, the completed milestone utilizes raycasting to render cubes on the screen, each representing a voxel. One significant benefit of this approach is the constant processing time required to render a frame, assuming the ray depth remains constant, regardless of the number of voxels on the screen. This rendering technique is particularly relevant to the milestone due to its efficiency in handling large amounts of microgeometry.


For instance, if we attempted to render a 3D scene with cubes (voxels) at half their size, the rasterization technique would need to process twice as many cubes. In comparison, the raycasting technique would not experience such an increase because there is one ray per screen-resolution pixel. Aside from these changes, lighting and texturing have been improved, as well as terrain generation.

Given the points mentioned above, I believe I have satisfied the following outcomes with this milestone:

Therefore, I have no updates regarding the feasibility of reaching all outcomes.

Some major concepts I learned during the development of this milestone include raycasting, ray-based texturing, ray-based diffuse lighting, terrain generation, 3D textures, and texture sampling, among others. There were many challenges, with the toughest being tracking down the causes of graphical artifacts. This is particularly difficult because the shader language lacks a console for logging results, leading to a lot of trial and error. Additionally, I have not yet developed a solution to update only the newly generated terrain, which forces me to resubmit all parts of the terrain to the GPU every time the camera moves to a new chunk. This is highly inefficient and something I intend to address.
